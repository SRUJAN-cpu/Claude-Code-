# One-Day Marathon Race Day Nutrition Plan

Here is a one-day meal plan for an athlete preparing for marathon race day with optimal carbohydrate timing:

* **Calorie Target:** Approximately 3,200 calories
* **Macronutrient Breakdown:** Protein (120g), Fat (80g), Carbs (520g)

**Meal Plan:**

* **Breakfast (6:00 AM):** Pancakes (150g cooked) with honey (40g), scrambled eggs (2 large), and orange juice (250ml).
    * Protein: 18g, Fat: 12g, Carbs: 75g

* **Pre-Run Snack (7:30 AM - 30 minutes before start):** Banana (120g) with white bread (50g) and honey (20g).
    * Protein: 3g, Fat: 1g, Carbs: 65g

* **During Race (Miles 5-20, every 45 minutes):** Sports drink (500ml total) and energy gels (3 packets, 30g each).
    * Protein: 2g, Fat: 0g, Carbs: 120g

* **Post-Run Recovery Meal (Within 30 minutes of finish):** Chocolate milk (500ml) with a bagel (90g) and turkey slices (60g).
    * Protein: 35g, Fat: 8g, Carbs: 95g

* **Lunch (2:00 PM):** Pasta with marinara sauce (250g cooked pasta, 200ml sauce), grilled chicken breast (150g), and garlic bread (60g).
    * Protein: 42g, Fat: 18g, Carbs: 85g

* **Afternoon Snack (4:30 PM):** Rice cakes (50g) with peanut butter (25g) and raisins (40g).
    * Protein: 10g, Fat: 15g, Carbs: 50g

* **Dinner (7:00 PM):** White rice (150g cooked), lean ground beef (140g), steamed carrots (150g), and olive oil (15ml).
    * Protein: 32g, Fat: 18g, Carbs: 60g

* **Evening Snack (9:00 PM):** Malt beverage (250ml) and pretzels (40g).
    * Protein: 8g, Fat: 8g, Carbs: 75g

This meal plan emphasizes high-quality carbohydrates for glycogen loading, strategic timing around the race, adequate protein for muscle recovery, and easily digestible foods to support optimal marathon performance.
